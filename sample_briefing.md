# EUV Chip-Making Supply Chain Faces Severe Bottlenecks Driven by Export Controls and Single-Supplier Concentration

## Top Concern: EUV Lithography Scanners (Risk: Critical)

The most advanced chip-making machines — EUV scanners made by ASML — are at serious risk of supply disruption through a compounding chain of constraints. At the foundation, **ultra-pure silica** feeds into **precision EUV optics and mirrors**, which are manufactured exclusively by Zeiss. Zeiss's tightly limited output is already the binding constraint on how many scanners ASML can build, with High-NA systems expected to remain in the low tens per year through 2028. On top of this physical bottleneck, U.S. and Dutch export controls fully ban EUV scanner and optics shipments to China, and ASML's multi-year backlogs continue to grow as AI-driven fab expansions from TSMC, Intel, and Samsung push demand well beyond production capacity.

In short: one supplier makes the critical mirrors, production is already maxed out, and export restrictions further limit where finished machines can go.

---

## Other Risks

- **Precision EUV optics and mirrors** *(Critical)*: Zeiss's sole-supplier status and constrained High-NA production independently bottleneck the entire EUV ecosystem through at least 2028.
- **EUV photoresist** *(High)*: Japan restricted high-end photoresist exports to dozens of Chinese companies in late 2025; Shin-Etsu and Tokyo Ohka — controlling ~80% of global supply — have suspended some deliveries.
- **Semiconductor-grade neon** *(Moderate)*: Residual tightness from Ukraine conflict persists; Chinese dual-use controls add indirect pressure, though no neon-specific ban is confirmed.
- **Ultra-pure silica** *(Low)*: No export controls currently in force; modest supply pressure from Strait of Hormuz disruptions noted.
